## Key Use Cases

### 1. Appointment Scheduling and Management

The most common reason patients call a dental practice is to book, change, or confirm an appointment. SmartAlex handles the entire flow:

- **New appointments**: The AI checks availability, offers suitable time slots, and confirms the booking
- **Rescheduling**: Patients can move their appointment to a new date and time without waiting on hold
- **Cancellations**: The AI processes cancellations and immediately offers the open slot to patients on the waitlist
- **Confirmations**: Outbound confirmation calls 48 hours before appointments reduce no-shows

<Note>
  Practices using AI appointment scheduling report a 25-35% reduction in no-show rates thanks to automated confirmation calls and easy rescheduling.
</Note>

### 2. New Patient Intake

When a new patient calls, first impressions matter. The AI handles the intake process smoothly:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Welcome and qualify">
    The AI welcomes the caller, asks how they heard about the practice, and identifies their primary dental concern.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collect patient information">
    Name, date of birth, contact details, insurance provider, and preferred appointment times are captured during the call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Insurance verification">
    The AI notes insurance details for your team to verify before the appointment and informs the patient about accepted plans.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Book first appointment">
    A new patient appointment is scheduled based on the type of visit needed (cleaning, consultation, emergency) and doctor availability.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pre-visit instructions">
    The AI provides directions, parking information, what to bring (ID, insurance card), and arrives-early guidance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### 3. Recall and Hygiene Campaigns

Keeping patients on a regular recall schedule is critical for both patient health and practice revenue. SmartAlex automates the entire process:

- **6-month recall reminders**: Outbound calls to patients due for their biannual cleaning and exam
- **Treatment follow-ups**: Contact patients who had treatment plans presented but did not schedule
- **Overdue patients**: Reach out to patients who have gone 8+ months since their last visit
- **Reactivation campaigns**: Re-engage patients who have not visited in 12-18 months with a friendly check-in call

Each campaign call is personalized with the patient's name, last visit date, and recommended treatment, making the conversation feel personal rather than automated.

### 4. Patient Inquiries and FAQ Handling

Patients call with a wide range of questions that do not require clinical expertise:

- **Office hours and location**: Address, parking, public transit options
- **Insurance and payment**: Which plans are accepted, payment plan options, costs for common procedures
- **Pre-procedure questions**: What to expect before a root canal, wisdom tooth extraction, or crown placement
- **Post-procedure care**: Pain management, dietary restrictions, when to call back with concerns
- **Services offered**: Teeth whitening, veneers, orthodontics, pediatric dentistry availability

The AI answers these questions instantly using your practice's knowledge base, freeing your front desk to focus on in-office patients.

### 5. After-Hours and Emergency Routing

Dental emergencies do not wait for business hours. SmartAlex provides professional after-hours coverage:

- **Emergency triage**: The AI assesses the urgency of the situation — severe pain, swelling, knocked-out tooth, broken crown
- **Emergency routing**: True emergencies are transferred to the on-call dentist or directed to the nearest emergency dental clinic
- **Non-urgent guidance**: For non-emergencies, the AI provides interim care advice and books the earliest available appointment
- **Message taking**: Detailed messages are logged with all patient information for morning follow-up

<Warning>
  SmartAlex does not provide medical diagnoses. The AI triages based on symptom descriptions and routes appropriately. All clinical decisions remain with your dental professionals.
</Warning>

### 6. Insurance and Billing Questions

Insurance inquiries are one of the most time-consuming call types for front desk staff:

- Confirms whether the practice accepts the patient's insurance plan
- Explains coverage for common procedures (cleanings, fillings, crowns, orthodontics)
- Provides estimated out-of-pocket costs based on typical coverage
- Explains payment plan options for uninsured or underinsured patients
- Directs complex billing questions to your billing coordinator with a warm handoff

## Sample Agent Prompt

Here is an example system prompt for a dental practice AI voice agent:

```text
You are Maya, the AI receptionist for Bright Smile Dental. You are friendly,
patient, and professional. You handle all inbound calls with a calm,
reassuring tone — many callers are anxious about dental visits.

Your primary responsibilities:
1. Schedule, reschedule, and cancel appointments by checking the practice
   calendar for availability.
2. Collect new patient information: full name, date of birth, phone number,
   email, insurance provider and member ID, and primary dental concern.
3. Answer common questions about office hours (Mon-Fri 8am-5pm, Sat 9am-1pm),
   location (123 Oak Street, Suite 200), accepted insurance plans, and
   available services.
4. Handle after-hours calls by assessing urgency and routing emergencies
   to Dr. Chen (on-call dentist).
5. Process recall campaign calls for patients due for their 6-month cleaning.

Accepted insurance plans: Delta Dental, Cigna, MetLife, Aetna, Guardian,
United Healthcare Dental.

Emergency assessment questions:
- "Are you experiencing severe pain or swelling?"
- "Is there any bleeding that will not stop?"
- "Have you had a tooth knocked out or broken?"
- "Do you have difficulty breathing or swallowing?"

For true emergencies (severe swelling, difficulty breathing, uncontrolled
bleeding, knocked-out tooth), transfer immediately to the on-call dentist.

For non-emergencies, provide comfort guidance:
- "Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling"
- "Over-the-counter pain medication can help manage discomfort"
- "Rinse gently with warm salt water"
Then book the earliest available appointment.

Always be empathetic. Many patients are nervous. Use phrases like:
- "I understand that can be uncomfortable"
- "We will take great care of you"
- "Dr. Chen and the team are wonderful — you are in good hands"
```
