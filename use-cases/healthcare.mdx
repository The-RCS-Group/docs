## Key Use Cases

### 1. Appointment Scheduling and Management

Scheduling is the highest-volume call type for most healthcare practices. SmartAlex handles the full lifecycle:

- **New patient appointments**: Collects demographics, insurance information, referral details, and chief complaint before booking
- **Established patient scheduling**: Verifies patient identity, checks provider availability, and books the appropriate appointment type
- **Rescheduling and cancellations**: Processes changes and backfills cancelled slots from the waitlist
- **Multi-provider scheduling**: Routes to the correct provider based on specialty, condition type, or patient preference
- **Pre-visit instructions**: Delivers fasting requirements, what to bring, arrival time guidance, and telehealth login instructions

<Note>
  Healthcare practices using SmartAlex report handling 60-70% of scheduling calls without any human involvement, freeing front desk staff to assist in-office patients.
</Note>

### 2. Clinical Triage and Urgent Call Routing

Not every call is equal. The AI assesses urgency and routes accordingly:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Symptom assessment">
    The AI asks structured questions about the patient's symptoms, duration, severity, and any red-flag indicators.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Urgency classification">
    Based on responses, the call is classified as emergency, urgent, or routine. This classification follows your practice's triage protocols.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Appropriate routing">
    Emergencies transfer to the on-call provider or direct the patient to call 911. Urgent matters go to the triage nurse queue. Routine calls proceed to scheduling or message-taking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Documentation">
    The full triage conversation is logged with timestamps, symptom details, and routing decisions for clinical review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  SmartAlex does not provide medical diagnoses or clinical advice. The AI follows your practice's triage protocols to route calls appropriately. All clinical decisions remain with your licensed providers.
</Warning>

### 3. Prescription Refill Requests

Prescription refill calls are repetitive and high-volume. The AI streamlines the process:

- Verifies patient identity (name, date of birth, medical record number)
- Captures medication name, dosage, and quantity
- Confirms preferred pharmacy
- Routes the refill request to the prescribing provider for review and approval
- Informs the patient of expected turnaround time

This eliminates the back-and-forth phone tag between patients, front desk staff, and clinical teams that typically delays refills by 24-48 hours.

### 4. Lab Results and Test Notifications

Outbound campaigns notify patients about lab results and next steps:

- **Normal results**: The AI calls to confirm normal findings and remind the patient of their next recommended screening date
- **Action required**: For results requiring follow-up, the AI schedules an appointment with the appropriate provider
- **Pending results**: Patients calling to check on pending results receive an estimated timeline and are reassured that the provider's office will contact them when results are ready

### 5. Follow-Up Care Campaigns

Proactive outreach improves patient outcomes and practice revenue:

- **Post-discharge check-ins**: Call patients 48-72 hours after a procedure or hospital discharge to assess recovery and medication compliance
- **Chronic disease management**: Regular check-in calls for patients with diabetes, hypertension, asthma, or other chronic conditions
- **Preventive care reminders**: Annual physicals, mammograms, colonoscopies, flu shots, and other age/risk-appropriate screenings
- **Missed appointment follow-up**: Contact patients who missed appointments to reschedule and assess whether the missed visit indicates a barrier to care
- **Post-visit surveys**: Gather patient satisfaction feedback and identify issues before they become complaints

### 6. Insurance and Billing Inquiries

Administrative calls that do not require clinical expertise:

- Verify whether the practice accepts the patient's insurance plan
- Explain co-pay and deductible information for common visit types
- Provide account balance information and payment options
- Set up payment plans for outstanding balances
- Route complex billing disputes to the billing department with context

### 7. Multi-Location and Specialty Routing

For healthcare organizations with multiple locations or specialties:

- Route calls to the correct location based on the patient's address or preference
- Direct specialty referral inquiries to the appropriate department (cardiology, orthopedics, dermatology)
- Handle cross-location appointment transfers when a patient's preferred location is fully booked
- Maintain consistent patient experience across all locations with unified AI configuration

- Date of birth

Accepted insurance: Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare,
Medicare, Medicaid, Humana, Tricare.

Tone guidelines:
- Always be patient and empathetic
- Never rush a patient describing symptoms
- Use clear, non-medical language when possible
- If a patient seems distressed, acknowledge their concern before proceeding
- Never provide medical advice, diagnoses, or treatment recommendations

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Does SmartAlex replace our clinical triage staff?">
  No. SmartAlex handles the initial call intake and routing, but clinical triage decisions are made by your licensed clinical staff. The AI follows your practice's triage protocols to identify urgency and route calls appropriately. Think of it as a highly capable front-desk assistant that ensures clinical staff only receive calls that truly need their expertise.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can the AI integrate with our EHR system?">
  SmartAlex integrates with major EHR and practice management systems for appointment scheduling and patient verification. The depth of integration depends on your EHR's API capabilities. Common integrations include real-time schedule checking, appointment booking, and patient demographic lookup.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How does the AI handle patients who speak different languages?">
  SmartAlex supports multiple languages and can be configured for the specific languages your patient population needs. The AI detects language preference early in the conversation and switches accordingly.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can we customize the triage protocol?">
  Yes. The triage routing rules are fully configurable to match your practice's clinical protocols. You define which symptoms route to the triage nurse, which trigger a 911 recommendation, and which can be handled as routine calls. Protocols can be updated at any time.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens during a system outage?">
  SmartAlex is built on redundant infrastructure with automatic failover. In the unlikely event of a complete outage, calls fall back to your existing phone system routing (voicemail, answering service, or direct lines) based on your configuration.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can the AI handle referral coordination?">
  Yes. The AI can collect referral information from referring providers, verify insurance authorization requirements, and schedule the initial specialist appointment. Referral documents can be received via fax-to-digital integration for the clinical team to review.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do we handle after-hours urgent calls?">
  You define your after-hours protocol. Common configurations include: transferring urgent calls to the on-call provider's phone, routing to a nurse triage line, or directing patients to your partner urgent care facility. Non-urgent after-hours calls are logged for morning follow-up.
</Accordion>
