## Key Use Cases

### 1. After-Hours Call Handling

Your business may close at 5pm, but your customers do not stop calling:

- **Evening and weekend inquiries**: Callers get immediate answers about your services, pricing, hours, and location instead of voicemail
- **Appointment booking**: Customers book the next available slot even when you are closed
- **Emergency routing**: Urgent calls (burst pipe for a plumber, locked out for a locksmith) transfer to your mobile or on-call number
- **Message taking**: Detailed messages with callback requests are waiting for you when you start your day

<Note>
  Small businesses that answer after-hours calls capture 35-40% more new customers than those that rely on voicemail. Most callers will not leave a message — they will call the next business on the list.
</Note>

### 2. Appointment Booking and Management

For service-based businesses — salons, spas, clinics, consultants, trades, fitness studios — appointment scheduling is the core of your revenue:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Customer calls">
    The AI greets the caller professionally and asks how it can help.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Service selection">
    The caller describes what they need. The AI identifies the correct service type and duration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Availability check">
    The AI checks your calendar for open slots and offers the best options based on the caller's preferences.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Booking confirmation">
    The appointment is confirmed with date, time, service, and any preparation instructions. Both you and the customer receive confirmation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reminder call">
    An automated reminder call 24 hours before the appointment reduces no-shows by up to 40%.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### 3. Customer Inquiry Handling

Most customer calls are asking the same 10-15 questions. The AI handles them instantly:

- **Business hours and location**: Including directions, parking, and public transit options
- **Services and pricing**: What you offer, how much it costs, and how long it takes
- **Availability**: When you can fit them in and current wait times
- **Policies**: Cancellation policies, payment methods accepted, warranties, and guarantees
- **How it works**: Your process from inquiry to delivery, what customers need to bring or prepare

You configure the AI with your business knowledge once, and it delivers consistent, accurate answers to every caller.

### 4. Order Status and Tracking

For businesses that take orders, process jobs, or manage projects:

- Customers call to check on their order, repair, or project status
- The AI provides updates based on your system data: received, in progress, ready for pickup, shipped
- For custom work (auto repair, tailoring, printing), the AI provides estimated completion dates
- If the customer needs to speak with someone, the AI schedules a callback at a convenient time

### 5. Callback Scheduling

When calls come in during busy periods or when specialized knowledge is needed:

- The AI offers to schedule a callback at a time that works for both the customer and your team
- Callback requests include the customer's name, number, reason for calling, and preferred time
- Your team sees all pending callbacks in one place, prioritized by urgency and time
- The customer gets a confirmation and knows exactly when to expect the return call

### 6. Outbound Marketing Campaigns

SmartAlex is not just for answering calls. Small businesses use outbound campaigns to drive revenue:

- **Promotional campaigns**: Announce sales, seasonal offers, new products, or special events to your customer list
- **Appointment reminders**: Reduce no-shows with automated reminder calls before scheduled appointments
- **Re-engagement**: Contact customers who have not visited in 3-6 months with a personalized offer
- **Review requests**: After a completed service, ask satisfied customers to leave a Google or Yelp review
- **Customer surveys**: Quick post-service satisfaction surveys help you improve and catch issues early
- **Referral campaigns**: Ask happy customers if they know anyone who could use your services

### 7. FAQ and Knowledge Base

The AI becomes your most knowledgeable employee:

- Answers detailed questions about your products, services, and processes
- Handles comparison questions ("What is the difference between your basic and premium package?")
- Provides preparation instructions ("What should I bring to my first appointment?")
- Explains policies clearly and patiently, even when asked the same question for the hundredth time
- Updates instantly when you change your hours, pricing, or offerings

## Sample Agent Prompt

Here is an example system prompt for a small business AI voice agent:

```text
You are Sam, the AI assistant for Riverside Auto Repair. You are friendly,
helpful, and knowledgeable about our services. You speak in a casual but
professional tone — like a trusted neighbor who happens to know a lot about
cars.

Your primary responsibilities:
1. Answer questions about our services, pricing, and availability.
2. Book service appointments by checking the shop calendar.
3. Provide updates on vehicles currently in the shop.
4. Handle after-hours calls by taking messages and routing emergencies.
5. Run outbound calls for service reminders and completed work pickups.

Services and estimated pricing:
- Oil change: $49-79 (30 min, synthetic extra)
- Brake inspection: Free (30 min)
- Brake pad replacement: $150-300 per axle (2-3 hours)
- Tire rotation: $25 (30 min)
- Full inspection: $89 (1 hour)
- AC service: $129-249 (1-2 hours)
- Check engine light diagnostic: $89 (1 hour)
- Battery test and replacement: $25 test, $120-250 battery (30 min)

Business hours:
- Monday-Friday: 7:30am - 5:30pm
- Saturday: 8:00am - 1:00pm
- Sunday: Closed

Location: 450 River Road (behind the Shell station, plenty of parking)

Booking rules:
- Oil changes and tire rotations can be same-day if slots are available
- Brake work and diagnostics need at least 1 day advance booking
- Major repairs are quoted after inspection

When taking appointments, collect:
- Customer name and phone number
- Vehicle year, make, and model
- Service needed
- Any symptoms or concerns ("making a grinding noise when braking")
- Preferred drop-off time

For vehicles currently in the shop:
- Ask for customer name or phone number to look up their vehicle
- Provide status: waiting for inspection, diagnosed, parts ordered,
  in progress, ready for pickup
- Give estimated completion time if work is in progress

After-hours emergency (tow needed, stranded):
- Provide our towing partner's number: (555) 987-6543
- Take a message for Mike (owner) to call back first thing in the morning

Tone: Be real. "Yeah, that grinding noise is usually brakes — better to get
that checked sooner rather than later." Be honest about pricing: "I can give
you a ballpark, but we will know the exact cost after we take a look."
```
